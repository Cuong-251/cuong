Consumer Insights

Investigating Tiki & Shopee

My Role

Project Leader

Timeline

Jan - Mar 2020

Insert Banner Image 1 Here

1. Executive Summary

An analytical approach to scaling a fast-casual brand in a hyper-competitive market. We identified a core misalignment in consumer perception and pivoted the brand from discount-focused to experience-oriented, resulting in a forecasted growth trajectory in brand equity.

2. Problem Statement

Otoké Chicken faced stagnation in market share. While product quality was high, the brand was trapped in a "price war" with larger competitors. The central business challenge was to increase brand loyalty without increasing marketing spend on traditional discounts.

3. Consumer Understanding

Through qualitative research, we discovered that customers viewed the dining experience as a "communal event" rather than a "quick meal." This insight revealed that our customers were seeking social validation and shared experiences, which were entirely missing from our previous marketing collateral.

4. Strategic Direction

We pivoted from broadcast advertising to community-based storytelling. By leveraging micro-influencers and local community hubs, we created a brand narrative focused on "the dining moment." This shift moved the brand from being a commodity to a community staple.

5. Expected Results

We project a 15% increase in repeat customer frequency and a 20% improvement in brand sentiment scores within 6 months. By shifting towards organic social growth, we anticipate a 10% reduction in customer acquisition costs, directly impacting the brand's long-term profitability.
